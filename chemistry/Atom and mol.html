Atoms and Molecules in Chemistry

Introduction

Chemistry is the study of matter, its properties, and the changes it undergoes. At the heart of chemistry are atoms and molecules, the fundamental building blocks of all substances.

Atoms: The Fundamental Units of Matter

Definition of an Atom

An atom is the smallest unit of an element that retains its chemical properties. It consists of a nucleus containing protons and neutrons, surrounded by electrons.

Bohr’s Atomic Model

Electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed energy levels or shells and can jump between levels by absorbing or releasing energy.

Structure of an Atom

  • Protons (positively charged, in the nucleus)
  • Neutrons (neutral, in the nucleus)
  • Electrons (negatively charged, orbiting the nucleus)

Atomic Number and Mass Number

  • Atomic Number (Z): Number of protons in an atom.
  • Mass Number (A): Total number of protons and neutrons.

Isotopes

Isotopes are atoms of the same element with different neutron numbers. Example: Carbon-12 and Carbon-14.

Molecules: The Chemical Units

Definition of a Molecule

A molecule is a group of two or more atoms chemically bonded together.

Types of Molecules

  • Diatomic (e.g., O₂, H₂)
  • Triatomic (e.g., H₂O, CO₂)
  • Polyatomic (e.g., CH₄, C₆H₁₂O₆)

Types of Chemical Bonds

  • Covalent Bond – Sharing of electrons (e.g., H₂O, CH₄)
  • Ionic Bond – Transfer of electrons (e.g., NaCl)
  • Metallic Bond – Free movement of electrons (e.g., Cu, Fe)

Laws of Chemical Combination

  • Law of Conservation of Mass: Mass remains constant in a chemical reaction.
  • Law of Definite Proportions: A compound always has the same proportion of elements by mass.
  • Law of Multiple Proportions: Elements combine in whole-number ratios.

Mole Concept

Definition of a Mole

One mole contains 6.022 × 10²³ particles (Avogadro’s number).

Molar Mass

  • H₂: 2 g/mol
  • O₂: 32 g/mol
  • CO₂: 44 g/mol

Example Calculation

18 g of H₂O contains 6.022 × 10²³ molecules.

Chemical Reactions and Equations

Balancing Chemical Equations

Example: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Types of Chemical Reactions

  • Combination: A + B → AB
  • Decomposition: AB → A + B
  • Displacement: A + BC → AC + B
  • Double Displacement: AB + CD → AD + CB

Conclusion

Atoms and molecules form the basis of chemistry. The mole concept and chemical laws explain how substances interact and transform.
