Chapter 2: Algebra and Equations

Introduction

Algebra is a branch of mathematics that helps us describe relationships using symbols and variables. It allows us to solve problems and understand patterns.

Understanding Variables

In algebra, we use letters to represent numbers. For example, in the equation:

x + 5 = 10

Here, "x" is an unknown number.

We can find "x" by solving the equation.

Basic Algebraic Operations

  • Addition: x + 3 = 8 → x = 8 - 3 = 5
  • Subtraction: x - 4 = 6 → x = 6 + 4 = 10
  • Multiplication: 3x = 12 → x = 12 ÷ 3 = 4
  • Division: x ÷ 2 = 7 → x = 7 × 2 = 14

Solving Simple Equations

Example: Solve 2x + 3 = 11

Step 1: Subtract 3 from both sides → 2x = 8

Step 2: Divide by 2 → x = 4

Practice Problems

  • 1. x + 7 = 14
  • 2. 5x = 25
  • 3. 3x - 2 = 10
  • 4. x ÷ 4 = 6
