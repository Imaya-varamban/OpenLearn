Chapter 1: Numbers and Arithmetic

Introduction

Mathematics starts with numbers and the fundamental operations that we use in daily life. In this chapter, we will explore different types of numbers and how to perform basic arithmetic operations on them.

Types of Numbers

  • Natural Numbers: The counting numbers (1, 2, 3, …).
  • Whole Numbers: Natural numbers including 0 (0, 1, 2, 3, …).
  • Integers: Whole numbers and their negatives (-3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, …).
  • Fractions: Numbers in the form of a/b, where b ≠ 0.
  • Decimals: Numbers with a decimal point (e.g., 3.14, 0.75).

Basic Arithmetic Operations

Arithmetic consists of four basic operations:

  • Addition (+): Combining two numbers.
  • Subtraction (-): Finding the difference between two numbers.
  • Multiplication (×): Repeated addition.
  • Division (÷): Splitting into equal parts.

Examples

Example 1: Addition

5 + 3 = 8

Example 2: Subtraction

10 - 4 = 6

Example 3: Multiplication

7 × 2 = 14

Example 4: Division

20 ÷ 5 = 4
