Chapter 3: Geometry and Measurements

Introduction

Geometry is the study of shapes, sizes, and the properties of space. Measurements help us determine lengths, areas, and volumes of different objects.

Basic Geometric Shapes

  • Triangle: A shape with three sides and three angles.
  • Square: A shape with four equal sides and four right angles.
  • Rectangle: A shape with opposite sides equal and four right angles.
  • Circle: A round shape with all points equidistant from the center.

Measuring Length, Area, and Volume

We use different units to measure geometric properties:

  • Length: Measured in millimeters (mm), centimeters (cm), meters (m), and kilometers (km).
  • Area: The space inside a shape, measured in square units (cm², m²).
  • Volume: The space a 3D object occupies, measured in cubic units (cm³, m³).

Perimeter and Area Formulas

Perimeter of a Rectangle: P = 2(l + w)

Area of a Rectangle: A = l × w

Area of a Triangle: A = ½ × base × height

Circumference of a Circle: C = 2πr

Practice Problems

  • 1. Find the area of a rectangle with length 8 cm and width 5 cm.
  • 2. Find the perimeter of a square with side length 6 cm.
  • 3. Calculate the circumference of a circle with radius 7 cm.
  • 4. Find the area of a triangle with base 10 cm and height 4 cm.
